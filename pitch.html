Why It Happens

Peer review was never
designed to verify data.

Reviewers evaluate plausibility — not reproducibility. The checks that matter most happen years later, if at all.

  • Reviewers rarely have access to raw data or code
  • Statistical errors (scale mismatches, pseudoreplication) are invisible in prose
  • Independent replication costs $50k–$500k and takes 6–24 months
  • Most papers are never formally replicated at all

"Peer review is a filter with holes. The data hiding in the supplementary files is where the truth lives — and nobody checks it."

— Synthesised from reproducibility literature

The Pipeline

Four stages.
Total isolation.

No analyst sees another's work until synthesis. Anchoring bias has nowhere to travel.

1
Data Collector
Fetches paper text + all supplemental data from OSF, Zenodo, GitHub, Dryad. Code files skipped.
2
Method Extractor
Strips author identifiers. Extracts every claim, test, preprocessing step, and ambiguity into a blinded brief.
3
3× Analysts
Analyst A replicates. B stress-tests. C audits assumptions. Running in parallel — never communicating.
4
Synthesis Judge
Cross-validates all three reports and issues a final verdict with a 5-dimension scorecard.
